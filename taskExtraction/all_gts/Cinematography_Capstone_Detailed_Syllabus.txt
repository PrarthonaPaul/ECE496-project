Course Description:
This capstone course focuses on the development of a cinematographic style for a short film or
commercial. Students will plan and execute a visual style, including lighting, camera movement, and
color grading, culminating in a screening or presentation at the Innovation Showcase. The course
includes the development of a project proposal, visual research, shot planning, and a final report.
Course Objectives:
Develop and implement a cinematographic style for a short film or commercial.
Plan and execute lighting techniques, camera movements, and visual style.
Enhance the visual style through post-production and color grading.
Effectively present the cinematographic project at the Innovation Showcase.
Learning Outcomes:
Gain expertise in cinematography and visual storytelling.
Develop practical skills in lighting, camera operation, and color grading.
Improve communication skills through written reports and presentations.
Enhance the ability to manage cinematography projects from conception to completion.
Group Project and Required Subtasks:
The group project for this course will involve the development of a cinematographic style for a short
film or commercial. The project will be broken down into the following subtasks:
1. **Project Proposal (Week 3):**
- Create a proposal detailing the visual style, lighting techniques, and camera movements to be
used in the project. Include a timeline and assign roles to team members.
2. **Visual Research and Shot Planning (Weeks 4-6):**
- Conduct visual research, including analysis of films and photography that align with the desired
style. Develop a shot plan, including camera setups, lighting diagrams, and shot lists.
3. **Principal Photography (Weeks 7-10):**
- Conduct the principal photography, implementing the planned visual style and lighting
techniques. Capture all necessary shots according to the shot list.
4. **Post-Production and Color Grading (Weeks 11-12):**
- Edit the footage, focusing on enhancing the visual style through color grading and visual effects.
Ensure that the final product aligns with the proposed cinematographic style.
5. **Final Report and Presentation (Weeks 13-15):**
- Document the entire cinematography process, including challenges, solutions, and outcomes in a
final report.
- Prepare a screening or presentation for the Innovation Showcase that highlights the key aspects
of the cinematographic style.
Groups are expected to collaborate closely, meeting regularly to discuss progress and resolve any
issues. Instructor check-ins will be scheduled to provide guidance and feedback.
