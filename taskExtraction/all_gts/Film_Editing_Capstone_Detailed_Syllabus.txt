Course Description:
This capstone course focuses on the editing of a short film or documentary. Students will edit a film
project, including footage review, rough cut, fine cut, and final cut, culminating in a screening or
presentation at the Innovation Showcase. The course includes the development of a project
proposal, rough cut assembly, and a final report.
Course Objectives:
Edit a short film or documentary from rough cut to final cut.
Review footage and assemble a narrative structure with pacing and continuity.
Develop sound design and apply color grading to enhance the visual style.
Effectively present the edited film at the Innovation Showcase.
Learning Outcomes:
Gain expertise in film editing and post-production.
Develop practical skills in narrative structure, sound design, and color grading.
Improve communication skills through written reports and presentations.
Enhance the ability to manage film editing projects from conception to completion.
Group Project and Required Subtasks:
The group project for this course will involve the editing of a short film or documentary. The project
will be broken down into the following subtasks:
1. **Project Proposal (Week 3):**
- Create a proposal detailing the editing style, narrative goals, and target audience for the project.
Include a timeline and assign roles to team members.
2. **Footage Review and Rough Cut (Weeks 4-6):**
- Review all footage and select the best takes for the rough cut. Assemble the rough cut, focusing
on narrative structure and pacing.
3. **Fine Cut and Sound Design (Weeks 7-10):**
- Refine the rough cut into a fine cut, addressing issues with continuity, pacing, and scene
transitions. Develop the sound design, including dialogue, sound effects, and music.
4. **Color Grading and Final Cut (Weeks 11-12):**
- Apply color grading to enhance the visual style of the film. Finalize the edit, ensuring that all
elements are polished and cohesive.
5. **Final Report and Presentation (Weeks 13-15):**
- Document the entire editing process, including challenges, solutions, and outcomes in a final
report.
- Prepare a screening or presentation for the Innovation Showcase that highlights the key aspects
of the edited film.
Groups are expected to collaborate closely, meeting regularly to discuss progress and resolve any
issues. Instructor check-ins will be scheduled to provide guidance and feedback.
