Course Description:
This capstone course focuses on the production of a documentary film, from research to final cut.
Students will develop a documentary project, including research, pre-production, principal
photography, and post-production, culminating in a screening or presentation at the Innovation
Showcase. The course includes the development of a project proposal, research, and a final report.
Course Objectives:
Produce a documentary film from research to final cut.
Conduct interviews, archival research, and location scouting for the documentary subject.
Edit the documentary, including sound design and color grading.
Effectively present the documentary at the Innovation Showcase.
Learning Outcomes:
Gain expertise in documentary filmmaking and storytelling.
Develop practical skills in research, shooting, and editing.
Improve communication skills through written reports and presentations.
Enhance the ability to manage documentary projects from conception to completion.
Group Project and Required Subtasks:
The group project for this course will involve the production of a documentary film. The project will
be broken down into the following subtasks:
1. **Project Proposal (Week 3):**
- Create a proposal detailing the subject matter, target audience, and narrative goals for the
documentary. Include a timeline and assign roles to team members.
2. **Research and Pre-Production (Weeks 4-6):**
- Conduct research on the documentary subject, including interviews, archival research, and
location scouting. Plan the production, including the narrative structure and shot list.
3. **Principal Photography (Weeks 7-10):**
- Conduct the principal photography, capturing interviews, B-roll, and other necessary footage.
Ensure that all elements are captured according to the narrative plan.
4. **Post-Production (Weeks 11-12):**
- Edit the documentary, including sound design, visual effects, and color grading. Finalize the
documentary for presentation.
5. **Final Report and Presentation (Weeks 13-15):**
- Document the entire production process, including challenges, solutions, and outcomes in a final
report.
- Prepare a screening or presentation for the Innovation Showcase that highlights the key aspects
of the documentary.
Groups are expected to collaborate closely, meeting regularly to discuss progress and resolve any
issues. Instructor check-ins will be scheduled to provide guidance and feedback.
